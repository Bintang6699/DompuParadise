Welcome to Dompu

Dompu Regency is one of the eight regencies in West Nusa Tenggara Province, Indonesia. Located on the eastern part of Sumbawa Island, Dompu offers breathtaking natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and warm hospitality.

With its diverse attractions ranging from majestic volcanoes and pristine beaches to traditional villages and historical sites, Dompu is emerging as one of Indonesia's promising tourist destinations.

History of Dompu

Tracing back the rich historical heritage of Dompu Kingdom

14th Century

Establishment of the Dompu Kingdom, one of the oldest kingdoms in Sumbawa region. The kingdom played a significant role in regional trade and politics.

17th Century

Spread of Islam in Dompu through traders and religious scholars from other parts of the archipelago. The kingdom adopted Islam as the official religion.

1815

The catastrophic eruption of Mount Tambora affected Dompu significantly, changing the landscape and impacting the kingdom's agriculture and settlement patterns.

1958

Formation of Dompu Regency as part of the administrative structure of the Republic of Indonesia, following independence.

Present Day

Dompu continues to develop while preserving its cultural heritage, with growing focus on sustainable tourism and economic development.

Culture & Traditions

Preserving ancient traditions while embracing modernity

Traditional Weaving

Dompu is renowned for its traditional woven fabrics, known for intricate patterns and natural dyes that reflect the local culture and environment.

Traditional Ceremonies

Various traditional ceremonies are still practiced, including rituals for agriculture, marriage, and other important life events, blending Islamic and local traditions.

Local Cuisine

Dompu's culinary traditions feature unique flavors with seafood specialties, traditional cakes, and dishes that reflect the region's agricultural products.

Geography & Nature

Dompu Regency features diverse landscapes including coastal areas, lowlands, and mountainous regions. The iconic Mount Tambora, known for its historic 1815 eruption, dominates the northern part of the regency.

The region boasts beautiful beaches like Lakey Beach, which is famous among surfers, and Satonda Lake, a unique volcanic crater lake with brackish water and rich biodiversity.

Key Natural Attractions:

  • Mount Tambora - Famous volcano with hiking opportunities
  • Lakey Beach - World-class surfing destination
  • Satonda Lake - Unique volcanic crater lake
  • Wadu Jao Peak - Scenic viewpoint with panoramic views
