About Dompu

Dompu Regency is one of the eight regencies in West Nusa Tenggara Province, Indonesia, located on the eastern part of Sumbawa Island. Known as the "Africa van Sumbawa," Dompu boasts breathtaking natural landscapes ranging from majestic volcanoes and pristine beaches to lush forests and serene lakes.

With its rich cultural heritage dating back to the ancient Dompu Kingdom, the region offers a unique blend of natural wonders and traditional customs. The local community is renowned for their warm hospitality, traditional weaving skills, and preservation of ancient rituals and ceremonies.

History of Dompu

Tracing the rich historical heritage of Dompu Kingdom

The Ancient Kingdom

Dompu has a remarkable history as one of the oldest kingdoms in the Sumbawa region, established around the 14th century. The Dompu Kingdom played a significant role in regional trade and politics, maintaining diplomatic relations with other powerful kingdoms across the archipelago.

During the 17th century, Islam spread throughout Dompu through traders and religious scholars, and the kingdom officially adopted Islam as its religion. This period marked the beginning of Dompu's unique cultural synthesis of Islamic traditions with local customs and beliefs.

The catastrophic eruption of Mount Tambora in 15 Juli 1815 significantly impacted Dompu, reshaping the landscape and affecting the kingdom's agricultural patterns and settlement distribution. Despite these challenges, Dompu's cultural heritage continues to thrive through traditional ceremonies, royal heirlooms, and architectural landmarks.
