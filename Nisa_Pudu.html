About Nisa Pudu Island

Nisa Pudu Island is one of the hidden natural tourism destinations in Dompu Regency, West Nusa Tenggara. This small uninhabited island offers the charm of a very pristine nature with fine white sand and crystal clear blue sea water.

The name "Nisa Pudu" comes from the Dompu language, where "Nisa" means island and "Pudu" or "Sapudu" means a handful, a pinch, or a small amount. It means, this island is "a small island like a handful".

Nisa Pudu Island is located in the middle of Saleh Bay waters, Soro Barat Village, Kempo District. To reach this island, visitors must rent a boat from Kempo Port with a travel time of about 30 minutes.

Access & How to Get There

How to Get to Nisa Pudu Island

Crossing Route

  1. 1 From Bima City, land travel to Kempo Port (¬± 2 hours)
  2. 2 From Kempo Port, rent a fishing boat to Nisa Pudu Island (¬± 30 minutes)
  3. 3 Boat rental cost: IDR 300,000 - IDR 500,000 (round trip, fits 6-10 people)

From Dompu City

  1. 1 From Dompu city center, head to Kempo District (¬± 1-1.5 hours)
  2. 2 Continue to Kempo Ferry Port
  3. 3 Rent a boat from the port

Visiting Tips

Preparation & Equipment

  • BRING your own food & drinks (no shops on the island)
  • Sunscreen, hat, and swimwear
  • Snorkeling equipment (optional)
  • Trash bag to bring your trash back

Best Time to Visit

  • Dry season (April - September)
  • Depart in the morning (07.00-09.00 WITA)
  • Return before 17.00 WITA
  • Avoid rainy season & western winds

Important Information

Operating Hours:

24 Hours (daytime recommended)

Entrance Fee:

FREE (only boat rental cost)

Accommodation:

No lodging on the island

Facilities:

Very limited, bring your own needs

Safety Tips

Always follow the advice from the boat guide regarding weather and sea conditions. Don't swim too far from the shore. Make sure the rented boat is in good condition and has basic safety equipment.
