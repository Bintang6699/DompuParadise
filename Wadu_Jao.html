About Wadu Jao Beach

Wadu Jao Beach is one of the hidden natural tourist destinations in Dompu Regency, West Nusa Tenggara, Indonesia. This beach offers natural charm that is still very pristine with soft white sand natural green stones and clear blue sea water.

The name "Wadu Jao" comes from the local language, where "Wadu" means stone and "Jao" means green. Legend has it that this name was given because there are large green stones around the beach that can be seen from a distance.

Wadu Jao Beach is not too crowded with tourists yet, making it a perfect place for those who want to enjoy tranquility and natural beauty without the disturbance of crowds.

How to Get to Wadu Jao Beach

From Outside Dompu

  1. 1 Fly to Sultan Muhammad Kaharuddin III Airport (SWQ) in Sumbawa
  2. 2 Take a travel/bus to Dompu (¬± 3 hours)
  3. 3 From Dompu City, continue to Wadu Jao Beach (¬± 45 minutes)

From Dompu City

  1. 1 From Dompu city center, head towards Woja District
  2. 2 Follow the road to Soro Village
  3. 3 There are signs to Wadu Jao Beach

Travel Tips

It is recommended to use a private vehicle or rent a vehicle because public transportation is limited. Bring water and food supplies if needed. Don't forget sunscreen and a hat to protect from the sun.
